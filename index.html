WARP DRIVE TUTOR: CHAPTER 1 — ALCUBIERRE DRIVE CONCEPTS Conceptual lessons

Learn the metric by answering.

The Alcubierre metric is special because it describes a warp bubble whose coordinate speed vs can exceed c, while the ship can remain locally at everyday speed inside.

Euclidean metric
ds2 = dx2 + dy2 + dz2

Plain spatial distance: no time, no curvature, no flow. Just geometry on a flat 3D grid.

Example If dx = 3, dy = 4, dz = 0: ds² = 3² + 4² + 0² = 25 so ds = 5 units.
Alcubierre warp metric
ds2 = −dt2 + (dx − vs f(rs)dt)2 + dy2 + dz2

The −dt2 term adds time, and the (dx − vsf(rs)dt)2 term replaces ordinary dx with dx minus a position-dependent x-coordinate shift between time slices.

θ expansion scalar
θ = vs x/rs df/drs

θ is not a term inside the metric. It is computed from the metric. It tells us whether tiny nearby volumes of space expand, contract, or stay neutral.

Sign example For motion in +x, vs > 0 and df/drs < 0 in the wall. ahead: x > 0 → θ < 0 contraction behind: x < 0 → θ > 0 expansion Same wall gradient, opposite side of the bubble, opposite sign.

The Alcubierre metric describes a moving warp bubble; Einstein's equations tell us what must source it.

The Alcubierre drive is usually introduced by writing down a desired spacetime geometry, then asking Einstein's field equations what kind of stress-energy tensor would be needed to make that geometry real.

Einstein field equations
Gμν = 8πTμν

Matter and energy tell spacetime how to curve; curved spacetime tells matter how to move. With the required Tμν, the metric can describe a bubble whose coordinate speed vs exceeds c, without making the ship locally outrun light.

Details Gμν is the geometry side: it is calculated from the curvature of the chosen metric, here the Alcubierre metric. Tμν is the stress-energy side: energy density, momentum flow, pressure, and stress. For a chosen warp metric, the equation tells you what Tμν would be required to source it.
Metric first, source inferred
metric → curvature → required Tμν

Define the spacetime you want, then calculate the stress-energy tensor needed to support it.

Calculation path Start with the metric gμν and inverse metric gμν. Compute Christoffel symbols Γρμν from first derivatives of gμν. Use them to compute the Riemann tensor Rρσμν. Contract Riemann to get the Ricci tensor Rμν. Contract again to get the Ricci scalar R. Build the Einstein tensor Gμν = Rμν - 1/2 Rgμν. Then infer Tμν = Gμν / 8π.
Why the ship rides along
locally calm interior + geodesic motion

In general relativity, freely falling matter follows geodesics. The ship can simply follow a geodesic inside the locally calm bubble interior, while the bubble geometry changes the relation between that interior and far-away space.

Source visualization

Tμν is a field over spacetime

The stress-energy tensor is not one number for the whole ship. It is Tμν(t, x, y, z): a tensor assigned to each event in spacetime. For the Alcubierre geometry, the important nontrivial structure is concentrated in the bubble wall.
